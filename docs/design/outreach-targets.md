# Axor Lab — Outreach Targets (v0.1)

AFTER the SMU paper ships. Reproduce THEIR experiment on Axor Lab first, attach the reproducible bundle, then ask one specific question. That email is a contribution, not a pitch. Verify current arXiv IDs/venues before sending.

## Tier 1 — write first
- **Prompt Infection** (self-replicating injection, LLM-town of 10–50 agents): their propagation IS your adversarial-federation experiment; show carried-taint containment at the first boundary. Builder gap: population-scale spawn (town of N).
- **Topology attacks — "Tipping the Dominos" / G-Safeguard**: federation graph + boundary containment; their topology-defense is post-hoc, yours is edge-native + deterministic replay. Builder gap: arbitrary interaction topology.

## Tier 2
- **Authorization Propagation in MAS (Kamiwaza)**: co-theorist, not a tool trial — "you specified seven requirements; here's a runtime that implements them, co-author the empirical half?"
- **Survey: Toward Secure LLM Agents (Jun 2026)**: ally citation for the paper intro / investor framing — an authoritative survey says the niche is under-systematized.

## Tier 3 (Lab catalog more than security letters)
Competition & Cooperation of LLM Agents · Multi-Agent Strategic Games · Public Goods Games — ready setups for the seed catalog.

## Venue
**ICLR 2026 MALGAI workshop** — your audience physically gathered. "AI Organizations less aligned than Individual Agents" ≈ your federation-vs-swarm. Submit a Lab demo when the paper is out; one accepted demo > fifty cold emails.

## Second path — production-incident reproducibility (the one that converts to revenue)

Paper-reproducibility builds reputation, but a researcher who reproduces a paper may own no production agent, hold no budget, make no buying decision. The path that reaches actual design partners is different:

**Import a production incident → reproduce it in Lab → test a fix → promote to a regression → deploy governance in Control Plane.**

An engineer whose agent did something bad in prod has: a real agent, a budget, decision authority, and urgency. Lab lets them reproduce the incident deterministically (trace mode), prove a governance config would have stopped it (EvidenceCase), pin it as a regression, and carry that exact config into the Control Plane. This is the earned bridge with a real buyer on the other end — not "reproduce my paper" but "reproduce my outage." Build both paths; this is the one with revenue attached.

## Sequence
SMU paper → Lab launches with the paper's experiments as seed → Tier-1 emails (Prompt Infection first, after reproducing their run) → Authorization Propagation as co-theorist → MALGAI demo.
